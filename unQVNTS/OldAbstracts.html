Speaker: Greg Warrington
Title: Overview of Kazhdan-Lusztig polynomials
Abstract: In 1979, Kazhdan and Lusztig constructed certain representations of the Hecke algebra associated to a Coxeter group W in order to elucidate representation-theoretic questions concerning W itself. To do this, they introduced a class of polynomials now known as the Kazhdan-Lusztig polynomials. These polynomials were immediately seen to have important interpretations in the context of Lie theory and Schubert varieties. In this talk I'll give an overview of Kazhdan-Lusztig polynomials while highlighting (as time and my knowledge allow) general connections in geometry and representation theory.
